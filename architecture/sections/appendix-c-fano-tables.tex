\section{Fano tables: fabric, mediators, syndrome}
\label{app:fano}

All tables are machine-verified (\texttt{domain\_transfer\_verify.py},
\texttt{talos\_collapse\_uniqueness.py}).

\paragraph{The seven line-buses.} The fabric of \S\ref{sec:fabric}:
$\{A,S,L\}$, $\{A,D,E\}$, $\{A,O,U\}$, $\{S,D,O\}$, $\{S,E,U\}$, $\{D,L,U\}$,
$\{L,E,O\}$. Each sector lies on exactly three; each pair on exactly one.

\paragraph{The mediator map.} The table-free reroute of \S\ref{sec:fabric}:
for each pair $(i,j)$, the mediator $k^\ast(i,j)$ is the third sector of their
line, and the only path by which their coherence regenerates.

\paragraph{The syndrome decode.} The zero-overhead fault localisation of
\S\ref{sec:ecc}. Label the seven sectors $1\dots7$ by the nonzero vectors of
$\mathbb{F}_2^3$. A single fault at sector $p$ produces a three-bit syndrome
equal to the binary of $p$; the three parity checks that fire are the three
lines through $p$. Twenty-one pair-relations collapse to seven axes, read by
three syndrome bits, to one verdict --- the pyramid $21\to7\to3\to1$.

\paragraph{The uniqueness arithmetic.} Theorem~\ref{thm:unique}:
$q^2+q+1=2^r-1$ has integer solutions $q\in\{2,5,90\}\Rightarrow
N\in\{7,31,8191\}$, of which only $q=2,5$ (i.e.\ $N=7,31$) are prime powers
and hence genuine projective orders ($q=90$ is not a prime power). The
division-algebra imaginary dimensions are $\{0,1,3,7\}$; the non-associative
one is $7$ alone; the triple intersection is $\{7\}$.
